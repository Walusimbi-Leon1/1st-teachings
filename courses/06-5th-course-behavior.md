### 5th Course (Behavior)


#### 1st Topic (Goals)


**1.5.1.1. "Goals", as you hear the word, "Goals", very exciting and full of life, it is worthy what you think it is, and if we explore it spiritually, it is very interesting. What is a Goal, that shouldn't be the major question, but the question is what does "Goal" do spiritually? Sinful acts, bad acts, distress, emotionally low, all this is under "Goals". Especially emotionally down, have you ever been there and you feel that life is a messy, when things are unpleasing, when you have joy, but when emotions are down. A goal is something or a task that a person sets in order to complete it. Goals are called emotion breakers simply because they destroy all kinds of negative emotions.**

**1.5.1.2. To apply "Goals", you should write things that you would like to complete, don't give duration to these Goals, no, they should be flexible. These Goals can intervene the Daily Schedule, yes, Goals can disrupt the Daily Schedule (DS), I mean that you can decide to disobey the Daily Schedule (DS) and you replace it with a goal, this is what makes Goals great. The Daily Schedule could not control or cover short time emotions, These are negative emotions that come for a short period of time, but they may come daily or weekly or monthly. Goals help in fighting these short time emotions.**

**1.5.1.3. So you Should write everything that you would like to complete, these things should be good, Goals should be pleasing, that everytime you start a goal, the negative emotions disappear. Goals should be pleasing that whenever you feel that some activities in the Daily Schedule are not pleasing for you to do, you instead do a goal in that time.**

**1.5.1.4. You create a spreadsheet called Goals, there you start writing in all the goals that you would like to complete, you may group them like creative goals, physical goals, and other groupings according to your likeness.**
